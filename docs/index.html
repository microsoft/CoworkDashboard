What this is, and why it helps

Cowork ROI is a two-part Microsoft Copilot Cowork solution that shows your team the value they're getting from Copilot — and turns it into a tidy report that lands in everyone's inbox.

  • Each teammate runs the “member” skill. It looks at their own Copilot Cowork work and posts a private, plain-language summary — hours saved, the dollar value of that time, the kinds of work, and what they produced — into one shared Teams channel.
  • You run the “manager” skill. It reads that channel, combines everyone's summaries into a single anonymized dashboard, and emails your team a tidy newsletter (an HTML dashboard plus a one-page guide) on the schedule you choose.
  • Private by design. People's names, file names, and prompts are never shared — only totals and categories, and nothing is ever shown at an individual level.

Both skills need to know the one Teams channel your team uses for these reports. This page bakes that channel into both downloads for you, so nobody is ever asked to paste a link — just follow the steps below.

1

Paste your team's Teams channel link

Where do I find this link in Teams?
  1. Open Microsoft Teams (desktop or web) and go to the channel you created for Cowork reports (e.g. Cowork report - {team}).
  2. Hover the channel name in the left list and click the ⋯ More options button that appears to its right (or right-click the channel).
  3. Choose Get link to channel (older Teams calls it Copy link).
  4. In the dialog, click Copy, then paste it into the box above.

The link looks like https://teams.microsoft.com/l/channel/19%3A…%40thread.tacv2/…?groupId=…. A plain chat link or a browser address-bar URL won't contain the groupId — use Get link to channel so it does.

“Verify” checks the link right here in your browser — it pulls out the team_id, channel_id and channel name, confirms the channel id is a real …@thread.… id and the team id is a valid GUID, and shows them below for you to confirm. It does not contact Teams or sign you in; nothing is sent anywhere. You can download either skill without this step — but adding a verified channel bakes it into the zip so no one is asked for a link.

3

Share the team-member skill — “Cowork ROI - Team Member Cowork Skill”

Send to team Team-member skill — Cowork ROI - Team Member Cowork Skill

No channel added in step 1 yet — this will be a generic copy that asks each teammate for the channel link on first run. Add your channel in step 1 to bake it in.

Send that team-member zip to everyone whose Copilot work you'd like included — over Teams, email, wherever works. Each person uploads it in Copilot Cowork — the .zip file as-is; don't unzip it first — and asks it to install the skill, the same simple step you just did:

Because your channel is already baked in, no one on your team is ever asked for a link — their reports simply start posting to your channel. When each teammate installs it, the skill asks whether to run once or automate every 15 days — have them choose to automate, so their reports keep posting on their own and the whole system stays hands-off.

Running this yourself too? If you (the manager) also want your own Copilot work counted in the team numbers, install this team-member skill on your own Copilot Cowork as well — that part's optional.

4

That's it — here's what happens next

  1. Each teammate runs the member skill, and their private summary posts to your shared channel.
  2. Your manager skill reads those posts, builds one anonymized dashboard, and emails your team a newsletter with the dashboard and a one-page guide attached.
  3. Keep it automated: your manager skill runs on its schedule (about every two weeks), and each teammate's member skill posts on its own 15-day schedule — together they keep the picture current with nothing to re-run by hand.

The manager skill only ever reads what teammates choose to post — it never touches anyone's files, and no individual is named in the dashboard or the email.
