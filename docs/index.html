Set up Cowork Dashboard for your team

A pair of Microsoft Copilot Cowork skills that measure the time and money your team saves with Copilot Cowork — your team's real ROI — and turn it into a private, easy-to-read team report emailed to you. This page bakes your Teams channel into both skills so they install ready to go — with nothing for your team to set up.

What this is, and why it helps

Cowork Dashboard is a two-part Microsoft Copilot Cowork solution that shows your team the ROI they're getting from Copilot Cowork — the hours saved and the dollar value of that time — and turns it into a tidy report that lands in everyone's inbox.

  • Each teammate runs the “member” skill. It looks at their own Copilot Cowork work and posts a private, plain-language summary — hours saved, the dollar value of that time, the kinds of work, and what they produced — into one shared Teams channel.
  • You run the “manager” skill. It reads that channel, combines everyone's summaries into a single anonymized dashboard, and emails your team a tidy newsletter (an HTML dashboard with the how-to-read guide built in) on the schedule you choose.
  • Private by design. People's names, file names, and prompts are never shared — only totals and categories, and nothing is ever shown at an individual level.

Both skills need to know the one Teams channel your team uses for these reports. This page bakes that channel into both downloads for you, so nobody is ever asked to paste a link — just follow the steps below.

1

Create a dedicated Teams channel for this rollup

Before anything else, create a brand-new, dedicated Teams channel just for these Cowork ROI reports, and add only the members you want included (they'll need access to post their summaries here).

  1. In Microsoft Teams, pick a team and choose ⋯ More options → Add channel (or Manage team → Add channel). Give it a clear name like Cowork report - {team}.
  2. Add only the members you want in this rollup — everyone you add can see and post here, so keep it to the people whose Copilot Cowork work should be counted.
  3. Once it exists, grab its link for the next step: hover the channel name, click ⋯ → Get link to channel, and click Copy.

🔒 Why a dedicated channel? A fresh, members-only channel keeps these reports private to the right people and gives the manager skill clean aggregation data — nothing else in the channel to wade through, so totals stay accurate.

2

Paste your team's Teams channel link

Where do I find this link in Teams?
  1. Open Microsoft Teams (desktop or web) and go to the channel you created for Cowork reports (e.g. Cowork report - {team}).
  2. Hover the channel name in the left list and click the ⋯ More options button that appears to its right (or right-click the channel).
  3. Choose Copy link (some Teams versions label it Get link to channel).
  4. In the dialog, click Copy, then paste it into the box above.

The link looks like https://teams.microsoft.com/l/channel/19%3A…%40thread.tacv2/…?groupId=…. A plain chat link or a browser address-bar URL won't contain the groupId — use Copy link so it does.

“Verify” checks the link right here in your browser — it pulls out the team_id, channel_id and channel name, confirms the channel id is a real …@thread.… id and the team id is a valid GUID, and shows them below for you to confirm. It does not contact Teams or sign you in; nothing is sent anywhere. You can download either skill without this step — but adding a verified channel bakes it into the zip so no one is asked for a link.

5

That's it — here's what happens next

  1. Each teammate runs the member skill once to start it, and their private summary posts to your dedicated channel — on its own 15-day schedule from then on.
  2. Your manager skill reads those posts, builds one anonymized dashboard, and emails your team a newsletter with the dashboard attached — the how-to-read guide is built right into it.
  3. Keep it automated: your manager skill runs on its schedule (about every two weeks), and each teammate's member skill posts on its own 15-day schedule — together they keep the picture current with nothing to re-run by hand.

The manager skill only ever reads what teammates choose to post — it never touches anyone's files, and no individual is named in the dashboard or the email.
